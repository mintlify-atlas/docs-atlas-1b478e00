## USB Power Delivery (PD)

The following devices are compatible with hardware requiring Power Delivery support (≥30W). Successfully tested with TS80P in PD mode.

### Important Requirements

- PD can only be provided through **USB-C to USB-C** cable
- Not only the charger but the **cable itself** must be capable of carrying higher wattages

### Compatible Devices for TS80P

| Device Name | IronOS FW |
|-------------|:---------:|
| Traver Charger QC09 (45W max)* | OK |
| Xiaomi AD65GEU Mi 65W Fast Charger with GaN Tech (AD65GEU, 65W max) | OK |

\* Available as an option with TS80P from [official store](https://aliexpress.com/item/4000764937427.html) or [NovelLife store separately](https://aliexpress.com/item/4001316262433.html) on AliExpress.

<Warning>
DO NOT BUY cheap "fast chargers with QC/PD support" for a few dollars online (less than ~$10). Reviews show they are often fake - even if lucky, you'll likely get 5V/1A max.
</Warning>

## Power Calculations

Power delivered to the tip is determined by voltage and tip resistance:

| Type       | Volts| / | Tip Ω | = |  Amps | * | Volts | = | Watts |
| :--------: | :--: |:-:| :---: |:-:|:-----:|:-:| :---: |:-:|:-----:|
| USB QC3.0  | 9V   | / | 4.5 Ω | = |  2.0A | * |  9V   | = |  18W  |
| USB-C PD   | 12V  | / | 4.5 Ω | = |  3.0A | * |  12V  | = |  32W  |
| USB-C PD   | 20V  | / | 8.0 Ω | = |  2.5A | * |  20V  | = |  50W  |
| USB-C PD   | 20V  | / | 6.2 Ω | = |  3.2A | * |  20V  | = |  64W  |
| DC Barrel  | 24V  | / | 8.0 Ω | = |  3.0A | * |  24V  | = |  72W  |
| DC Barrel  | 24V  | / | 6.2 Ω | = |  3.8A | * |  24V  | = |  92W  |
| EPR PD3.1  | 28V  | / | 8.0 Ω | = |  3.5A | * |  28V  | = |  98W  |
| EPR PD3.1  | 28V  | / | 6.2 Ω | = |  4.5A | * |  28V  | = |  126W |

Use an [Ohm calculator](https://www.rapidtables.com/calc/electric/power-calculator.html#dc) to quickly derive watts for your configuration.

## Best Practices

1. **Use highest voltage possible**: Power delivery is proportional to voltage squared (P = V² ÷ Ω)
2. **PD adapters recommended**: Allows iron to know supply limitations and prevent over-current
3. **Check cable ratings**: Ensure USB-C cables are rated for the wattage you need
4. **Avoid ultra-cheap chargers**: Spend at least $10 for reliable performance
5. **Test power banks**: Some shut down below 50mA draw; IronOS handles this in most cases
